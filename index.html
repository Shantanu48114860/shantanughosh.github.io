Research

My research interests lie in computer vision, deep learning, causal inference, and their applications in medical image analysis focusing on basic to advanced architectures of supervised and unsupervised deep neural networks to improve their generalization and interpretability. I am particularly interested in developing explainable AI (X-AI) algorithms to (1) understand the rich internal structure of a blackbox deep neural network (2) improve the reasoning methodology in terms of high-level interpretable concepts in an image (3) bridge the gap between post-hoc explainability and interpretable by design methods.

Publications
Anatomy-Guided Weakly-Supervised Abnormality Localization in Chest X-rays
Ke Yu, Shantanu Ghosh, Zhexiong Li, Christopher Deible, Kayhan Batmanghelich
Proceedings of the International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2022
[paper] [code]

We develop a deep learning network, AnatomyGuided chest X-ray Network (AGXNet), which leverage the radiology reports to create an anatomy-guided attention module using the weak labels of the anatomy mentions in the report to localize the disease and anatomical abnormality.

DR-VIDAL-Doubly Robust Variational Information-theoretic Deep Adversarial Learning for Counterfactual Prediction and Treatment Effect Estimation
Shantanu Ghosh, Zheng Feng , Jiang Bian , Kevin Butler , Mattia Prosperi
AMIA Annual Symposium, 2022. Oral, to appear
[arXiv] [code] [supp]

We develop a novel deep learning framework which generates the counterfactual outcomes based on a treatment using Generative Adversarial Network and information theoretic regularization. Next, we utilize the counterfactual outcomes to estimate the individual treatment effect (ITE) using a novel deep learning network with doubly robust optimization for fast convergence.

Propensity score synthetic augmentation matching using generative adversarial networks (PSSAM-GAN)
Shantanu Ghosh, Christina Boucher, , Jiang Bian , Mattia Prosperi
Journal Computer methods and programs in biomedicine update
[paper] [code]

We develop a novel algorithm using Generative Adversarial Network to generate synthetic treated samples to remove imbalance within an observational dataset for Propensity score matching.

Deep propensity network using a sparse autoencoder for estimation of treatment effects
Shantanu Ghosh, Jiang Bian , Yi Guo , Mattia Prosperi
Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association
[paper] [code]

We develop a stacked autoencoder to reduce the dimensionality of the feature vectors of the patients to calculate the Propensity score in an efficient way to estimate average treatment effect (ATE) of the treatment.
